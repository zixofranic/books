# Chapter 6 — Anthropic, the Company That Broke Off Over Safety

**Version:** v1 · **Last updated:** 2026-05-30 · **Source:** *Just Predicting Words*, Ch6

---

## Founding

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by seven researchers who had left OpenAI. The founding group included Dario Amodei (formerly VP of Research at OpenAI) and his sister Daniela Amodei (now President of Anthropic), along with several other senior OpenAI alumni. The stated reason for the departure was a disagreement about safety — specifically, the view that OpenAI was moving too quickly relative to its safety research.

Anthropic was set up explicitly as a safety-focused AI lab. The plan was to participate at the frontier of capability so that safety research would be done on the same systems being deployed, rather than as a separate, slower track.

## Claude

Anthropic's main product is the Claude family of assistants. Claude was first released in March 2023. The Claude 2, Claude 3, and Claude 3.5 generations followed; by 2026, Claude is generally considered one of the two or three strongest frontier models in the world.

Claude is trained using Constitutional AI (see chapter four) and is known for being relatively careful, willing to push back on the user, and willing to acknowledge uncertainty.

## The Claude Code moment, May 2025

In May 2025, Anthropic launched Claude Code, an agentic coding tool designed for developers. Within months, it had generated over one billion dollars in annualized revenue — one of the fastest products to a billion in software history.

Within fifteen months of that launch, Anthropic's overall annualized revenue had jumped from a few hundred million to thirty billion dollars. Eighty times what it had been a year earlier.

## The valuation race

By 2026, Anthropic had become a roughly nine-hundred-billion-dollar company in private markets. Major backers include Google, which has committed up to forty billion dollars in investments, and Amazon, which has committed up to twenty-five billion. Anthropic has signed compute contracts with Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Broadcom, and — in a surprising arrangement — Elon Musk's SpaceX for additional capacity.

The full story of how a safety-focused lab founded by seven researchers became a nine-hundred-billion-dollar company in five years has not been fully told. The author's framing: faster than almost anyone outside its founders predicted, with all the complications that follow.

## The Pentagon designation, March 2026

In March 2026, the United States Pentagon designated Anthropic a supply chain risk and blacklisted the company from working with the military. The dispute arose after contract negotiations broke down. The Pentagon wanted unrestricted use of Claude for all lawful military purposes; Anthropic refused to permit uses like fully autonomous weapons and domestic mass surveillance. When the company missed the government's deadline, the administration moved to bar federal agencies from using its technology. Anthropic has been challenging the designation in court while continuing its broader growth.

## Anthropic's stated worldview, 2026

Anthropic publicly argues that AI capabilities will continue to grow rapidly, that the alignment problem (getting models to do what we actually want) is genuinely hard and not solved, that the technology should be developed by labs that take safety seriously rather than left to those who don't, and that being at the frontier is the price of being able to do meaningful safety research. The lab invests heavily in interpretability research — the effort to understand what is happening inside a model — because it believes that without interpretability, claims about safety cannot really be verified.

Dario Amodei has publicly predicted that AI could wipe out half of entry-level white-collar jobs within five years and push unemployment into double digits. He has also written extensively (notably in "Machines of Loving Grace," 2024) about the upside potential of safe AI for science and human welfare.

## What to remember about Anthropic in one paragraph

Founded 2021 by seven ex-OpenAI researchers, led by Dario and Daniela Amodei. Makes the Claude assistant. Constitutional AI is its alignment approach. Claude Code launched May 2025 and helped drive revenue from a few hundred million to thirty billion in fifteen months. Now valued near nine hundred billion. Major backers: Google up to forty billion, Amazon up to twenty-five billion. Pentagon designated it a supply-chain risk in March 2026 after Anthropic refused to permit autonomous-weapons and mass-surveillance uses; Anthropic is challenging in court.
