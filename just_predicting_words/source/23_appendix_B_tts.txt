A reference for the major moments in modern AI development.
Selected for readers who want to remember when things happened.
Not exhaustive.

Pre-History...

nineteen fifty-six - Dartmouth Workshop
The summer workshop where the term "artificial intelligence" was
first used. Organized by John McCarthy with Marvin Minsky,
Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon.

nineteen seventies - First AI Winter
Funding for AI research collapses across the US and UK after
early systems fail to meet expectations.

nineteen eighties - Second AI Winter
Another collapse after expert systems fail to scale.

twenty thirteen - word2vec
Tomas Mikolov and colleagues at Google demonstrate that word
meanings can be captured as vectors. King minus man plus woman
equals queen.

twenty seventeen - Attention Is All You Need
Eight researchers at Google publish the Transformer paper. The
foundation for modern language models.

The GPT Years...

twenty eighteen - GPT one...

Open AI releases its first GPT model. 117 million parameters.
An academic curiosity.

twenty nineteen - GPT two...

Open AI releases GPT two. 1.5 billion parameters. Initially withheld
as "too dangerous to release." Eventually released in November
twenty nineteen.

twenty twenty - GPT three...

Open AI releases GPT three. 175 billion parameters. The first model
that startled even its creators with what it could do. Available
only through an API.

The Chat GPT Moment...

twenty twenty-two, November 30 - Chat GPT Launches
Open AI launches Chat GPT, a simple chat interface to a fine-tuned
GPT three point five. The fastest-growing consumer product in history. 1
million users in 5 days. 100 million in 2 months.

twenty twenty-two to twenty twenty-three - The Scramble
Google declares a code red. Anthropic releases Claude. Meta
releases Llama as the first major open-weight model. Microsoft
deepens its investment in Open AI.

The Frontier Years...

twenty twenty-three, March - GPT four
Open AI releases GPT four, a major capability jump from GPT three point five.
Multimodal capabilities.

twenty twenty-three, March - Claude
Anthropic releases its first version of Claude.

twenty twenty-three, November - The Open AI Board Crisis
Sam Altman is fired by the Open AI board, then reinstated within
four days after employee revolt and Microsoft pressure.

twenty twenty-four - The Year of Models
Multiple major releases. GPT-4o. Claude three family. Gemini one point five.
Llama three. Frontier capabilities continue to expand.

twenty twenty-four, March - Mustafa Suleyman to Microsoft
In an unusual deal, Microsoft hires most of Inflection's team
and licenses the technology.

twenty twenty-four, August - EU AI Act
The European Union's AI Act becomes law. Implementation will
be staggered through twenty twenty-six and twenty twenty-seven.

twenty twenty-four - Demis Hassabis Nobel Prize
Hassabis and John Jumper share the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for
Alpha Fold's work on protein folding.

The Deep Seek Moment And Beyond...

twenty twenty-five, January - Deep Seek V three Release
A Chinese lab releases an open-weight model trained for $6M that
performs competitively with American frontier models. The Deep Seek
moment. Nvidia loses $500B in market cap in a single day.

twenty twenty-five, April - Llama four Disaster
Meta releases Llama four. The release is later found to have used
different model versions for different benchmarks, gaming the
results. Internal consequences are severe.

twenty twenty-five, May - Claude Code
Anthropic launches Claude Code, an agentic coding tool. Within
months, it generates over $1B in annualized revenue.

twenty twenty-five, November - Le Cun Leaves Meta
Yann Le Cun departs Meta to found AMI Labs, focused on world
models.

twenty twenty-five, December - Trump AI Executive Order
US President Trump signs an executive order directing the AG
to challenge state AI laws that conflict with a proposed
federal framework.

twenty twenty-five - Anthropic Catches Up
Anthropic's revenue grows from a few hundred million to tens of
billions over the year. The frontier gets multipolar.

The Present Moment (twenty twenty-six)...

twenty twenty-six, January - The twenty twenty-six International AI Safety Report
Yoshua Bengio chairs the report, warning about deepfakes, AI
companions, and increasing capability.

twenty twenty-six, February - Space X Acquires X AI
X AI becomes a subsidiary of Space X. The combined entity is
valued at $1.25 trillion.

twenty twenty-six, March - Meta Pivots to Closed Weights
Meta launches Muse Spark as its first closed-weight frontier
model, stepping back from its open-weight commitments.

twenty twenty-six, March - Anthropic Pentagon Designation
The US Pentagon designates Anthropic a supply chain risk after
the company refuses to remove restrictions on military use of
Claude.

twenty twenty-six, April - Deep Seek V four Release
Deep Seek releases V four trained largely on Huawei Ascend chips,
proving Chinese AI hardware has matured.

twenty twenty-six, May - The X AI / Space X Consolidation
Musk announces X AI will cease to exist as a separate company,
folded entirely into Space X's Space X AI division.

twenty twenty-six, May - EU AI Act Streamlining Agreement
EU institutions reach a provisional agreement to streamline
parts of the AI Act while maintaining substantive obligations.

twenty twenty-six, August (scheduled) - EU AI Act High-Risk Provisions
The bulk of the EU AI Act's obligations on high-risk systems
scheduled to take effect, with a contested proposal to delay
until twenty twenty-seven.
